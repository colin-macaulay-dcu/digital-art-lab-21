Talking Avatar Lessons

Students design a portrait, bring it to life using the Talkr app, and record a voiceover — combining visual art, digital literacy, and storytelling.

Tool: Talkr App1st – 6th ClassVisual ArtsCross-Curricular

Core Objectives

Across all four activities, students build the same core skills — applied to different contexts.

🎨

Creating

Design a portrait incorporating artistic elements — line, colour, texture — to reflect personality, era, or artistic style.

🎤

Performing

Animate the portrait using the Talkr app and record a voiceover script — combining oral language, storytelling, and digital skills.

🔍

Responding

Analyse the character's context — whether artistic, historical, or literary. Reflect on creative choices and engage in peer feedback.

Choose a Lesson

Each activity uses the same Talkr workflow but applies it to a different theme and class level.

1st – 2nd Class

Create a Character Profile: Roald Dahl's Willy Wonka

Bring a character to life by creating a digital talking portrait and recording their voice.

Students explore the character of Willy Wonka by creating a visual and narrative character profile. They design a portrait incorporating his unique traits and whimsical personality, then bring it to life using the Talkr app to animate and record a voiceover.

Lesson Plan↓ Download PDF

Learning Outcomes

1

Creative Expression: Students produce a personalised portrait showcasing Wonka's eccentric traits.

2

Literary Understanding: Students demonstrate understanding of the character's role in the story.

3

Digital Literacy: Students use animation and voice recording tools to bring their portrayal to life.

3rd – 4th Class

Create an Artist Profile: The Movement of Fauvism

Create a digital character profile of a Fauvist artist, combining their artistic style and personal story.

Students explore the bold art movement of Fauvism by creating a fictional artist inspired by its principles. They design a portrait using exaggerated colours and simplified forms, then animate their artist and record a voiceover.

Lesson Plan↓ Download PDF

Learning Outcomes

1

Creative Expression: A vivid Fauvist-inspired portrait capturing the essence of the movement.

2

Art Historical Understanding: Knowledge of Fauvism — bold colours, simplified forms, emotional expression.

3

Digital Literacy: Using animation and voice recording to bring a Fauvist artist to life.

3rd – 4th Class

Create a Titanic Passenger Profile

Create a digital talking portrait of an imaginary Titanic passenger, bringing their story to life.

Students create a fictional Titanic passenger — combining historical context with creative storytelling. They design a digital portrait considering class, background, and motivations, then animate their character using the Talkr app.

Lesson Plan↓ Download PDF

Learning Outcomes

1

Creative Expression: A personalised Titanic character portrait reflecting understanding of the era.

2

Historical Understanding: Knowledge of the Titanic's social structure and historical significance.

3

Digital Literacy: Using digital drawing and animation tools to design and animate a character.

5th – 6th Class

Create a Viking Warrior Profile

Bring a Viking warrior character to life by creating a digital talking portrait and sharing their story.

Students create a Viking warrior, explorer, or craftsperson — combining historical understanding with artistic expression. They design and paint a portrait using traditional art materials, then bring the character to life with the Talkr app.

Lesson Plan↓ Download PDF

Learning Outcomes

1

Creative Expression: A unique Viking character portrait showcasing bold artistic choices.

2

Historical Understanding: Knowledge of Viking roles, culture, and motivations through character design.

3

Digital Literacy: Animating and voicing a Viking character, combining traditional art with technology.

The 5-Step Workflow

Every lesson follows the same creative process.

01

Research

Explore the theme — study the character, era, or art movement.

02

Design

Sketch and plan the portrait — deciding on key visual elements.

03

Create

Make the portrait — using traditional or digital art materials.

04

Animate

Upload to the Talkr app and record a voiceover as the character.

05

Present

Share and discuss — peer feedback and reflection.
